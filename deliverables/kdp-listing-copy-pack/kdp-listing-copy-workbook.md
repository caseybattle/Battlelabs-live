# KDP Listing Copy Workbook

This workbook turns one rough KDP idea into a cleaner listing: title angles, subtitle options, bullet benefits, a structured description, keyword clusters, and compliance reminders.

This is a planning aid. No sales or ranking results are guaranteed.

## Step 0 — One-sentence buyer clarity

Write one sentence:

“This is for **[buyer/recipient]** who wants **[outcome]** in the context of **[use case/occasion]** using a **[format]** that is different because **[differentiator]**.”

## Step 6 — Buyer objection checklist

Answer in one sentence each:

1) Why should a buyer pick this instead of a generic alternative?  
2) What makes it trustworthy (without hype)?  
3) What is the simplest way to use it in one day?  
4) What would make a buyer hesitate (price, time, confusion)?  
5) What proof can you show (preview pages, examples, structure)?  

## Compliance + originality reminders

- Do not copy competitor listings, reviews, or trademarked phrases.
- Check platform rules before publishing.
- If AI assisted the content, follow the required AI-content disclosure rules for your platform (including KDP).
- You are responsible for final accuracy, compliance, and publishing decisions.

## Fictional before/after example (structure only)

Example product (fictional): “New Teacher Classroom Planner”

Before (too vague): “A planner for teachers to stay organized.”

After (clearer): “A first-year teacher classroom planner with weekly routines, parent communication logs, and a simple grading workflow—built for new teachers who want less chaos in their first semester.”
