# KDP Cover Direction Brief Plan

Status: Ready

## SKU

`KDP-COVER-001`

## Product Angle

KDP Cover Direction Brief

## Buyer

Self-publishers and low-content book creators who have a niche idea (or draft listing) but are stuck translating it into a clear, compliant cover direction that reads correctly at thumbnail size.

## Problem

Even a solid niche can look generic if the cover does not signal the audience, promise, and category expectations quickly. Most creators do not have a repeatable way to write a cover brief without copying competitor covers.

## Promise

Turn a rough KDP book, journal, or workbook idea into a cover direction brief: audience + promise, mood and category cues, typography direction, color constraints, imagery direction, and a thumbnail test checklist.

No sales, ranking, or approval outcome is guaranteed.

## Free Hook

Free Cover Direction Prompt + Checklist

The free hook should help the buyer define:

- buyer/recipient and use case,
- category/genre signals (without copying),
- mood words + color constraints,
- typography direction,
- imagery direction,
- and a thumbnail readability check.

## Paid Deliverable

Cover Direction Brief Workbook + Templates

Suggested intro price: `$9`

Regular target price: `$19`

Include:

- one-page cover brief worksheet,
- a mood/keywords-to-visual mapping table,
- typography + color direction sheet,
- imagery constraint prompts (what to include / avoid),
- thumbnail test checklist,
- KDP compliance reminder (trademarks, AI disclosure, category expectations),
- AI disclosure reminder,
- fictional example briefs only (no copied covers).

## Cross-Sell Logic

If a user already has a niche and listing angle, the next bottleneck is cover clarity and thumbnail readability.

This can be linked from the scorecard and listing-copy pages after the first version exists and evals pass.

## Compliance Notes

- Do not claim ranking improvements.
- Do not claim guaranteed sales.
- Do not encourage copying competitor covers or listings.
- Remind buyers to check trademarks and platform rules.
- Examples must be fictional and not derived from real covers.

## Kill / Improve Rules

Keep building if:

- the page gets checklist clicks,
- KDP pages get checkout clicks,
- or users ask about covers / "how should my cover look".

Improve if:

- checklist clicks occur but paid workbook clicks do not.

Kill or pause if:

- no KDP scorecard signal exists after the Product Hunt launch cycle,
- the page needs too much explanation,
- or compliance risk rises.
