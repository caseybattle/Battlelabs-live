# Async Teardown: Shay Johnson

## What is working
- The offer is clear and tied to a concrete transformation.
- The page likely attracts the right type of buyer for one-to-one launch support.
- The application/call motion probably works for the most motivated leads.

## Likely conversion leak
- The current path appears to rely too heavily on a full application or live step before there is an easier async sorter.
- Buyers at different readiness levels likely need different next steps, but the current flow may push them too quickly into one path.
- That can reduce both lead capture and follow-up quality.

## One change to make first
Add a readiness-based async qualification step:
- one shorter inquiry path for buyers who are interested but not ready
- one clearer route for ready-now leads
- one follow-up sequence tied to readiness level

## What Battle Labs would build
- readiness-based CTA cleanup
- short async inquiry form
- follow-up sequence for launch-ready vs not-yet-ready buyers
- simpler front-end routing before the full application or call

## Tight response version
I would start by separating ready-now buyers from interested-but-not-ready buyers before they hit the same full apply/call path. That usually creates a cleaner front-end flow and better follow-up without changing the core offer. If you want, I can scope that into a pilot build and keep the whole process async.
