# Async Teardown: Jonathan Dunnett

## What is working
- The page is direct and aligned with a speaking/coaching style business.
- The visitor is given a strong, obvious next step.
- Authority is not the problem; the likely issue is conversion handling before the call.

## Likely conversion leak
- The page appears to move interested visitors straight into a live booking step without a softer async option.
- That usually screens out warmer prospects who are interested but need a lower-friction way to raise their hand first.
- Speaker and consulting leads often need more context, examples, or qualification before a live slot feels appropriate.

## One change to make first
Add an async pre-call capture path:
- one “request the breakdown” or “tell me about your event/business” form
- one follow-up sequence for speaking vs coaching/consulting
- one short qualification layer so Jonathan can review better-fit leads first

## What Battle Labs would build
- a softer first CTA for visitors not ready to book live
- cleaner inquiry capture for speaking and consulting needs
- follow-up logic that keeps warm leads moving after the page visit
- optional page rewrite so the live call becomes the second step, not always the first

## Tight response version
Your authority is clear, so the bigger issue is not trust, it is the amount of friction before someone can enter your pipeline. I would start by adding a softer async capture path before the live booking step, plus follow-up tied to the type of inquiry. If you want, I can scope that into a pilot build and keep the whole process async.
