# Free KDP Listing Angle Checklist

Use this checklist to score whether your listing explains the idea clearly **before** you publish.

This is a planning aid. No sales or ranking results are guaranteed.

## How to use

1. Read each line and mark **Yes / Sort of / No**.
2. If you have more than **2 "No"** answers, rewrite the angle before you write keywords.
3. If you have more than **2 "Sort of"** answers, tighten the recipient + occasion + outcome.

## The checklist

### 1) Buyer clarity

- The buyer/recipient is specific (not “everyone”).
- The buyer has a reason to care *now* (occasion, problem, or habit).
- I can describe the buyer in one sentence without buzzwords.

### 2) Use case clarity

- The use case is obvious (what they do with it).
- The format is obvious (journal, workbook, planner, log, checklist, etc.).
- The size or scope is implied (quick guide vs. deep workbook).

### 3) Outcome clarity

- The outcome is a result the buyer wants (not a feature list).
- The outcome is believable and non-hype.
- The angle avoids “guaranteed” language, rankings, income, or approval claims.

### 4) Differentiation

- The differentiator is real (audience, structure, constraint, tone, or format).
- The differentiator does not depend on copying competitor listings.
- I can explain why mine exists in one sentence.

### 5) Trust + compliance

- The listing does not use trademarked phrases without permission.
- I understand the platform’s rules for metadata and descriptions.
- If AI assisted the content, I will follow the required AI-content disclosure rules for the platform.

## Next step

- 6–12 title angles,

- 7 bullet benefits,
- 1 structured description,
- and 3 keyword clusters (intent/recipient/occasion).
